# Board One-Pager: Action Acceptance Before Impact

## The issue

Agentic AI is moving from producing outputs to taking actions: invoking tools, changing records, deploying code, moving data, triggering workflows, initiating transactions, and affecting physical or regulated systems.

Traditional controls remain necessary, but high-consequence autonomy creates a narrower question:

> Should this exact action be accepted by this protected system right now?

## Why it matters

A valid identity can still perform a stale action. An attested runtime can still affect the wrong target. A prior policy check can become obsolete before impact. A log can help reconstruct harm but cannot always prevent it.

## The board-level risk

Organizations may deploy autonomy faster than they can prove why specific actions were accepted.

## The governance ask

Require every high-consequence AI initiative to identify:

1. protected actions;
2. acceptance boundaries;
3. evidence categories required at the gate;
4. ACCEPT / HOLD / REFUSE conditions;
5. owner for held/refused actions;
6. audit story for why accepted actions became real.

## The procurement ask

Add this question to AI infrastructure procurement:

> Can this system prove why an autonomous action was accepted before impact?

## The boundary

This public kit supports readiness and category literacy only. Production enforcement, cryptographic verification, conformance, certification, and domain integration require separate licensed, authorized, or standards-track work.
