# Contributing

Contributions should strengthen public understanding without disclosing implementation mechanisms.

## Good contributions

- New public scenario cards.
- Better RFP questions.
- Terminology clarifications.
- Human-impact and stewardship guidance.
- Domain-specific readiness questions.
- Public-interest adoption templates.
- Developer-experience improvements for the educational scenario-card linter.

## Do not contribute

- Production verifier code.
- Exact evidence or receipt schemas.
- Cryptographic binding details.
- Non-bypassable interceptor designs.
- Patent claim charts or claim mappings.
- Partner deployment details.
- Unpublished claim language.
- Signed conformance vectors.
- Certificate registry logic.
- Certification badges or conformance assertions.
- Any implementation-level material that would blur the public/private boundary.

Your contribution does not grant, request, imply, or require any patent license, trademark license, service mark license, product implementation license, certification right, compliance approval, conformance approval, endorsement, or right to use Meridian Verity Group names, logos, marks, or framework identifiers as source-identifying marks.

By contributing, you represent that you have the right to submit the material and that it does not include confidential information, partner-specific material, private implementation detail, unpublished patent claim language, claim charts, or controlled disclosure material.

## Public/private boundary

Contributions must not include implementation-level material, exact schemas, cryptographic mechanisms, non-bypassable enforcement designs, certificate-registry logic, signed conformance vectors, partner deployments, claim charts, or unpublished patent language.

All contributions must preserve the release boundary:

> Public category. Public readiness. Private mechanism.

Safe public materials define vocabulary, scenarios, discussion patterns, RFP questions, public-interest guidance, and education. Public contributions must not create an implementation guide, product specification, formal standard, certification program, conformance suite, or patent claim chart.

## Review standard

Maintainers should reject or request changes for any contribution that could reasonably be mistaken for:

- production readiness,
- compliance approval,
- certification,
- conformance approval,
- an implementation disclosure,
- a patent license,
- a trademark permission,
- a claim chart,
- a partner deployment statement, or
- a representation that any product is Acceptance Plane™ certified.
