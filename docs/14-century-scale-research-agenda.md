# Century-Scale Research Agenda

This agenda describes public research directions that could make action acceptance before impact a durable norm. It is aspirational and does not claim that this public kit solves these problems.

## 1. Action-acceptance literacy

Teach the difference between output correctness, access control, runtime protection, and accepted action. Build curricula for engineers, policymakers, auditors, clinicians, safety teams, civil-society groups, procurement leaders, and students.

## 2. Scenario commons

Grow a public corpus of educational scenarios where communities can discuss when autonomous actions should be accepted, held, or refused. Keep public scenarios readable and non-production; reserve conformance vectors for authorized paths.

## 3. Procurement norms

Make this question a default enterprise and public-sector buying question:

> Can this system prove why an autonomous action was accepted before impact?

## 4. Safety economics

Study how fail-closed action acceptance affects incident costs, insurance, operational resilience, patient safety, financial losses, public trust, and institutional accountability.

## 5. Privacy-preserving accountability

Develop public principles for proving that an acceptance decision was made under current, scope-bound evidence without exposing unnecessary personal data, proprietary implementation details, or sensitive infrastructure internals.

## 6. High-consequence domains

Explore public readiness profiles for healthcare, finance, public services, education, critical infrastructure, mobility, robotics, AR/VR, sensor-mediated environments, and model lifecycle operations.

## 7. Global accessibility

Design readiness materials that work for low-resource settings, intermittent connectivity, multilingual communities, small public institutions, and organizations without large security teams.

## 8. Democratic oversight

Help regulators, journalists, watchdogs, courts, auditors, schools, hospitals, and affected communities ask whether AI actions were accepted under clear authority, current evidence, and human-compatible accountability.

## 9. Intergenerational stewardship

Build systems our children can inherit with more confidence: autonomy that is powerful, useful, and fast where appropriate, but not unaccountable where consequences are real.

## Boundary reminder

This research agenda is public-good framing only. It does not disclose exact schemas, cryptographic protocols, production enforcement, conformance suites, certificate registries, domain adapters, private deployments, or patent claim maps.
