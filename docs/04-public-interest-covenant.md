# Public-Interest Covenant

The purpose of the Acceptance Plane public materials is to help protect people before autonomous AI actions become harm.

## Commitments

1. Human consequence comes before technical elegance.
2. High-consequence autonomy requires evidence before impact.
3. Logs after harm are not enough.
4. Affected people, institutions, and communities deserve systems that can explain why an action was accepted.
5. Public vocabulary should be shared widely.
6. Production mechanisms should be handled responsibly, licensed appropriately, and deployed with competent oversight.
7. Acceptance controls should not become a tool for deception, surveillance overreach, or false assurance.
8. Public materials should be clear about what they do and do not grant.

## Human-centered framing

The Acceptance Plane is not an anti-autonomy idea. It is a stewardship idea. The point is not to slow every action. The point is to prevent high-consequence actions from becoming real without sufficient proof.

## The 100-year principle

If autonomous systems become a major layer of civilization, the most important question will not only be whether machines can act. It will be whether human institutions built trustworthy boundaries around when those actions become real.
