# Stewardship and Human Impact

The Acceptance Plane is ultimately about people.

Autonomous systems can act on protected environments: health records, financial accounts, classrooms, public benefits, production infrastructure, vehicles, medical devices, sensors, cameras, and social workflows. In these environments, the cost of accepting the wrong action is not merely technical. It can be human, institutional, financial, civic, or physical.

## The stewardship question

Before an autonomous action becomes real, a protected system should be able to ask:

- Who or what is acting?
- Under what delegation?
- Against what target?
- Under what scope?
- Under what current policy state?
- With what runtime and contextual evidence?
- With what freshness and revocation posture?
- With what consequence?
- Why accept, hold, or refuse now?

## Design posture

The most humane autonomous systems will not be the ones that act fastest in every situation. They will be the ones that know when speed is safe, when ambiguity requires pause, and when proof failure must stop action.
