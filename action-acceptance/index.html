Dual-gate rail

The control moments are before inference and before external effect commitment.

Gate 0 asks whether a request may run. Gate 1 asks whether this specific external effect may commit.

01Execution requestOrigin, scope, context, and policy-epoch candidate.
Gate 0Deny before inferenceOrigin, constraints, entitlement, policy epoch, revocation, and required evidence must verify.
03Attested runtimeProtected posture: model, agent, tools, verifier evidence, and boundary context.
04Proposed effectEgress, secret release, privileged API, dispatch, output release, or regulated workflow step.
Gate 1Permit before commitAction digest, nonce, TTL, policy digest, revocation, and receipt boundary verify before impact.
06External interfaceThe effect is accepted only after proof.

Evidence rail: policy epoch + revocation state + verifier posture + nonce/challenge + action digest + DecisionReceipt + EvidencePack.

Decision semantics

Missing proof holds. Bad proof fails. Fresh proof accepts.

The decision sits before inference or before the external effect is accepted, not after impact.

ACCEPT

Fresh, scoped, unreplayed, machine-verifiable proof tied to the proposed action and active policy.

HOLD

Missing, stale, or insufficient proof that can be remediated by refresh, approval, assurance lift, or downstream enforcement.

FAIL

Invalid, replayed, mismatched, rolled back, expired, future-issued, or out-of-scope evidence.
